Our Vision

The Omni Collective shares a political vision—one that, at its most basic level, privileges a more equitable commoning of resources and meeting of human needs over private interests or corporate profit.

Omni Commons is a building in Temescal, Oakland, CA. It was built in the 1930s by an Italian-American garbage workers' union. They constructed a ballroom, bar, and boccee ball court. Over the years it also accumulated two industrial kitchens, a large basement library, and a disco dance hall. At some point in the 1980s the disco hall became a death metal club—they named it the Omni. After this music venue was closed, the Omni became a private residence. Until now.

The Omni Collective has pooled its resources and efforts to take this building off the market and turn it into a public commons, free for everybody. All of our groups believe in public service, accessibility, social justice, grassroots activism, civic engagement, and serving our many communities. Our groups all struggle or just can't afford to pay our city's increasing rent prices on our own, because we are not about the money. We can only survive if we join together.

Commons are desperately needed. In Temescal, in Oakland, in the SF Bay Area, in California, in the USA, and everywhere in our 21st century world.
